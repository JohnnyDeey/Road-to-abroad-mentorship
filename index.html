Welcome — Read This First

What this guide is and how to use it

Who This Is For

You are a Computer Science student at FUOYE who wants to study abroad for a Master's or PhD. Your family income is limited, so scholarships and funded programmes are not a bonus — they are the plan. This guide shows you it is entirely possible.

Good news: Canada (research MSc/PhD), Germany, Norway, and some UK universities offer fully funded graduate programmes. You don't need to be rich. You need to be strategic.

How to Use This Guide

Go through each Module in order. Use the Task List to check off items and add notes — John sees everything live and will give you feedback.

John Is Watching 👀

Every task you check, every note you write — John sees it in real time. Be honest, complete your tasks, and ask questions before your review sessions.

Key rule: Only apply to funded pathways. Unless funding is clearly identified, do not apply to self-funded programmes.

Module 1: Know Yourself First

Build your academic profile before anything else

🎓 CGPA

Most funded MSc programmes want 3.0+/5.0. A lower GPA can be overcome with strong projects and a great SOP. Write your current CGPA down honestly.

💻 Projects

List every coding project, final year project, or research you've done — no matter how small. These matter enormously for postgraduate applications.

📖 Publications

Any paper, even a conference poster? If not, your FYP topic can become your research proposal. Write the topic down now.

🌍 English Test

Most universities require IELTS 6.5+ or TOEFL iBT 90+. Plan your test date for Month 2–3. Free prep resources exist online.

Task: Open a Google Doc. Write a 1-page Academic Profile — your CGPA, all projects, FYP topic, any awards, and your research interests. Share the link with John in your task note below.

Module 2: Find the Right Programmes

Research universities and identify the right fit

Canada — Research-Based MSc/PhD (Funded)

The best route is a research-based (thesis) Master's. Your supervisor funds you through research grants — you receive a stipend and tuition waiver.

  • Memorial University of Newfoundland — lowest tuition, very international-friendly
  • University of Manitoba — lower cost of living, active funding
  • University of Alberta — strong AI research
  • Dalhousie University — funded research positions
  • University of Waterloo — world-class CS
Search tool: findamasters.com → filter Canada + CS + funded.

Germany — Near-Zero Tuition

Public universities charge only ~€150–350/semester. Many programmes are fully in English. DAAD scholarships can cover living costs.

  • TU Munich, TU Berlin, KIT Karlsruhe, University of Stuttgart
  • Search at: daad.de

Australia — Research Degrees + Scholarships

The Research Training Program (RTP) waives tuition for international PhD students. Check Monash, University of Melbourne, ANU, UNSW, University of Queensland.

Norway & Nordic — Free Tuition

Norway's public universities charge no tuition for anyone. You only cover living costs (~NOK 12,000/month). Also: Sweden (KTH), Finland (Aalto), Denmark (DTU).

Module 3: Find Professors & Read Research

The most important module for research-based programmes

Why This Matters

For funded research degrees, a professor must agree to supervise you before you are admitted. Without a willing supervisor, your application will likely be rejected even with excellent grades.

How to Find Them

  • Google Scholar: Search your topic → click an author name → see their university and all papers
  • ResearchGate: Search by topic, see recent publications
  • University CS department pages: Look for "Prospective Students" sections
  • IEEE Xplore / arXiv / ACM Digital Library: Find papers you like, look up the corresponding author

How to Read Their Paper

Read at least one paper from the last 2 years. Focus on: Abstract (what problem?), Introduction (why does it matter?), Future Work (what do they plan next? — this is what you offer to continue).

Free papers: Sci-Hub.se (paste the DOI), arXiv.org, or Google Scholar's free PDF links.
✉️

Module 4: How to Email a Professor

The email that can change your life — done right

The Golden Rules

  • Keep it short — 3–4 paragraphs maximum
  • Personalise every email — mention their specific paper by exact title
  • Show you actually read their work — one specific sentence about it
  • State your CGPA clearly. Attach your CV. Use your university email.
  • Follow up once after 2 weeks if no reply. Never more than once.
📧 Professor Email Template
Subject: Prospective Graduate Student — [Research Area] — [Your Name] Dear Professor [Last Name], I am [Your Name], a final-year Computer Science student at the Federal University of Oye-Ekiti (FUOYE), Nigeria, graduating in [Month Year] with a CGPA of [X/5.0]. I am writing to inquire about PhD/MSc research opportunities in your group starting [e.g. September 2026]. I recently read your paper "[Exact Paper Title]" ([Journal/Conference, Year]), and I found your approach to [specific aspect] particularly compelling. I am especially interested in extending this toward [your specific angle or their "future work"]. My background includes [1–2 sentences on your FYP or relevant project]. I believe this aligns well with your group's research direction. I have attached my CV. If you are currently accepting students, I would be glad to provide transcripts or a research statement at your convenience. Thank you for your time. Sincerely, [Your Full Name] Federal University of Oye-Ekiti (FUOYE) | B.Sc. Computer Science [Email] | [LinkedIn or GitHub]
Never say: "I came to know about you through the internet." | "I am hardworking and dedicated." | Copy-paste the same email to everyone. | Ask about funding in the first email.

Module 5: Scholarships for Nigerians

Realistic funding sources open to you

Strategy: Apply for both university funding (stipends, assistantships) AND external scholarships. Never rely on only one source.

🌍 Mastercard Foundation

Covers tuition, living, and travel for African students. One of the best for Nigerians. Deadline: Oct–Dec annually.

Module 6: Documents Checklist

Get these ready early — some take months

⚠️ Start passport and transcripts today. Nigerian passports take 6–12 weeks. Official transcripts take 4–8 weeks. Do not wait.

📘 International Passport

Apply immediately via the NIS portal. Allow 2–3 months. Book appointment, pay ~₦35,000, attend office.

NIS Portal

📋 Full List

  • Official transcripts — sealed copies from registry
  • Academic CV — education, CGPA, projects, skills, awards
  • 3 Letters of Recommendation — ask lecturers, give 6 weeks notice
  • English Language Certificate — IELTS, TOEFL, or Duolingo
  • Birth Certificate — certified copy
  • Research Proposal — 1–3 pages for PhD applications
  • Statement of Purpose (SOP) — see Module 7
✍️

Module 7: Writing Your SOP

The most important document in your application

SOP Structure — Follow This Exactly

  • Para 1 — Hook: Open with a specific CS problem. Not "I love computers." Instead: "The challenge of securing AI systems against adversarial attacks became real to me when..."
  • Para 2 — Background: Your degree, CGPA, most relevant projects. Use numbers.
  • Para 3 — Research Interest: What specific problem do you want to solve? Why does it matter?
  • Para 4 — Why This University/Professor: Name the professor. Mention their paper. Explain the fit. Never generic.
  • Para 5 — Future Goals: What will you do after the degree?
Task: Write a first rough draft — even if it's bad. Use the structure above. Paste the Google Doc link in your task note. John will review it.
Avoid: "I have always been passionate since childhood." | Listing your CV again. | Vague research interests. | Same SOP for every university — customise Para 4 each time.
🚀

Module 8: Submitting Applications

Apply smartly and track everything

How Many?

Apply to 5–8 universities: 2 stretch schools, 3–4 target schools, 2 safety schools. Prioritise universities where a professor already replied positively.

Per-University Checklist

  • Create account on the university's application portal
  • Upload transcripts (sealed) + CV + customised SOP
  • Enter referees' contact details
  • Upload English test scores
  • Pay application fee (always ask for a waiver first)
  • Submit before the deadline — never on the last day
Track every deadline. Canadian universities typically: January–February deadlines for September intake. Use a spreadsheet and set phone alarms 30 and 7 days before each.
🛂

Module 9: Visa & Pre-Departure

After you receive an offer

Never accept an offer without showing John first. Check carefully whether it includes full funding (tuition waiver + stipend) or requires self-funding.

Canadian Study Permit

  • Apply on the IRCC portal (ircc.canada.ca)
  • You need: acceptance letter, proof of funds (university funding letter counts), passport, biometrics, medical exam
  • Allow 2–4 months processing time
  • Book VFS Global appointment in Nigeria early
IRCC Portal

🏠 Housing

Apply for on-campus housing immediately after accepting your offer — it fills up fast.

✈️ Arrival

Book your flight only after visa is confirmed. Arrive before orientation. Contact student union for support.
