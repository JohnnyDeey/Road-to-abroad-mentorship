Welcome — Read This First

What this guide is and how to use it

Who This Is For

You are a Computer Science student at FUOYE who wants to study abroad — ideally in Canada, Australia, or Europe — for a Master's or PhD. Your family income is limited, so scholarships and funded programmes are not a bonus: they are the plan. This guide will show you it is entirely possible, and many Nigerians do it every year.

Good news: Many universities in Canada (research-based MSc/PhD), Germany, Norway, and some in the UK offer fully funded or heavily subsidised graduate programmes. You do not need to be rich. You need to be strategic.

How to Use This Guide

Go through each Module in order. Use the Task List at the bottom to check off items. Add notes and evidence links on each task — John can see them and will give you feedback.

John Is Watching 👀

John can see your progress in real time. Every task you check, every note you write — he sees it. Complete your tasks, write honest notes, and ask questions before your review sessions.

⚠️ Key rule: Only apply to funded pathways. Unless a scholarship or funding is clearly identified, do not apply to programmes that require self-funded tuition.

Module 1: Know Yourself First

Build your academic profile before anything else

Your Academic Profile

Before applying anywhere, write down your answers honestly:

🎓 CGPA

What is your current CGPA? Most funded MSc programmes want 3.0+/5.0 (60%+). A lower GPA can be overcome with strong research experience and an excellent SOP.

💻 Projects

List every coding project, final year project, or research work you've done, no matter how small. These matter enormously for postgraduate applications.

📖 Publications

Have you co-authored any paper — even a conference poster? If not, your FYP topic can become your research proposal. Write the topic down.

🌍 English Proficiency

Most universities require IELTS 6.5+ or TOEFL iBT 90+. Plan your test date in Month 2–3. Free preparation resources exist.

Task: Open a Google Doc and write a 1-page "Academic Profile" — CGPA, all projects, FYP topic, any awards, and your research interests. Share it with John for review.

Module 2: Find the Right Programmes

Research universities and identify the right fit

Canada — Research-Based MSc/PhD (Funded)

The best route is a research-based (thesis) Master's. Supervisors fund students through research grants — you receive a stipend and tuition waiver. Key universities for CS:

  • Memorial University of Newfoundland — lowest tuition in Canada, very international-friendly
  • University of Manitoba — lower cost of living, active funding
  • University of Alberta — affordable province, strong AI research
  • Dalhousie University — funded research positions
  • University of Waterloo — world-class CS
How to search: Go to findamasters.com or mastersportal.eu → filter Canada + Computer Science + funded.

Germany — Near-Zero Tuition

Public universities charge only ~€150–350/semester. Many programmes are fully in English. DAAD scholarships can cover living costs too.

  • TU Munich, TU Berlin, KIT Karlsruhe — top CS schools
  • University of Stuttgart, University of Hamburg
  • Search at: daad.de

Australia — Research Degrees + Scholarships

The Research Training Program (RTP) waives tuition for international PhD students. Check university-specific scholarships at Monash, University of Melbourne, ANU, UNSW, University of Queensland.

Norway & Nordic Countries — Free Tuition

Norway's public universities charge no tuition for anyone. You only cover living costs. Also consider Sweden (KTH, Uppsala), Finland (Aalto), Denmark (DTU).

  • University of Oslo, NTNU (Trondheim), University of Bergen

Module 3: Find Professors & Read Research

The most important module for research-based programmes

Why This Matters

For funded research degrees, a professor must agree to supervise you before you are admitted. Without a willing supervisor, your application will likely be rejected even with excellent grades.

Step 1 — Search for Professors

  • Google Scholar: Search your research interest (e.g. "machine learning network security"). Click an author name → see their university and all papers.
  • ResearchGate: Search by topic. See recent publications and activity.
  • University department websites: Go to the CS faculty page. Look for "Prospective Students" or "Graduate Students Wanted" links.
  • IEEE Xplore / ACM Digital Library / arXiv: Search your area. Find papers you like and look up the corresponding author.

Step 2 — Read Their Recent Paper

Before emailing, read at least one paper from the last 2 years. Focus on:

  • Abstract: What problem are they solving?
  • Introduction: Why does it matter?
  • Conclusion / Future Work: What do they plan to do next? This is what you offer to continue.
Get papers free: Use Sci-Hub.se (paste the DOI), Unpaywall browser extension, or Google Scholar's free PDF links.
✉️

Module 4: How to Email a Professor

The email that can change your life — done right

The Golden Rules

  • Keep it short — 3–4 short paragraphs maximum
  • Personalise every email — mention their specific paper by title
  • Show you actually read their work — one specific sentence
  • State your background and CGPA clearly
  • Attach your CV
  • Use your university email address
  • Follow up once after 2 weeks if no reply — never more
📧 Professor Email Template
Subject: Prospective Graduate Student — [Research Area] — [Your Name] Dear Professor [Last Name], I am [Your Name], a final-year Computer Science student at the Federal University of Oye-Ekiti (FUOYE), Nigeria, graduating in [Month Year] with a CGPA of [X/5.0]. I am writing to inquire about PhD/MSc research opportunities in your group starting [e.g. September 2026]. I recently read your paper "[Exact Paper Title]" ([Journal/Conference, Year]), and I found your approach to [specific aspect — 1 sentence] particularly compelling. I am especially interested in extending this work toward [your specific angle or the future work they mentioned]. My research background includes [1–2 sentences on your FYP or relevant project]. I believe this aligns well with your group's current research direction. I have attached my CV for your consideration. If you are currently accepting students, I would be glad to provide transcripts, a research statement, or a writing sample at your convenience. Thank you for your time. Sincerely, [Your Full Name] Federal University of Oye-Ekiti (FUOYE) | B.Sc. Computer Science [Email] | [LinkedIn or GitHub]
Do NOT: "I came to know about you through the internet." | "I am hardworking and dedicated." | Copy-paste the same email to all professors. | Ask about funding in the first email.

Module 5: Scholarships for Nigerians

Realistic funding sources open to you

Strategy: Apply for both (1) university-based funding (research stipends, assistantships) AND (2) external scholarships. Never rely on only one.

🌍 Mastercard Foundation Scholars

Covers tuition, living expenses, and travel for African students. One of the most powerful scholarships for Nigerians. Deadline: usually October–December annually.

🇩🇪 DAAD Scholarships (Germany)

Full scholarships for African students to study in Germany. Covers tuition, living allowance, travel. Highly respected.

🇨🇦 Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships

$50,000 CAD/year for 3 years for PhD students. Very competitive — apply if your CGPA and research experience are strong.

Module 6: Your Documents Checklist

Get these ready early — some take months

⚠️ Start your passport and transcripts today. Nigerian passports can take 6–12 weeks. Official transcripts from your university can take 4–8 weeks. Do not wait.

📘 Passport

Apply for your Nigerian international passport immediately via the NIS portal. Allow 2–3 months. Book appointment, pay fee (~₦35,000), attend office.

NIS Portal

📜 Academic Transcripts

Request official transcripts from your university registry — both sealed copies (for direct submission) and certified personal copies. Start now.

📋 Full Documents List

  • Academic CV — education, CGPA, projects, skills, awards
  • Statement of Purpose (SOP) — see Module 7
  • 3 Letters of Recommendation — ask lecturers who know your work. Give 6 weeks notice.
  • English Language Certificate — IELTS, TOEFL, or Duolingo English Test
  • Birth Certificate — certified copy
  • Research Proposal — 1–3 pages for PhD applications
✍️

Module 7: Writing Your Statement of Purpose

The most important document in your application

What is an SOP?

A 1–2 page essay where you explain: who you are, what you have done, what you want to research, and why this university/professor is the right fit. This is where you win or lose the admission decision.

SOP Structure — Follow This Exactly

  • Para 1 — The Hook: Open with a specific CS problem that excites you. Not "I have always loved computers." Instead: "The challenge of securing AI systems against adversarial attacks became real to me when..."
  • Para 2 — Your Background: Your degree, CGPA, most relevant projects. Be specific and use numbers.
  • Para 3 — Your Research Interest: What specific problem do you want to solve? Why does it matter?
  • Para 4 — Why This University/Professor: Name the professor. Mention their paper. Explain why their lab is where your research belongs. Never be generic.
  • Para 5 — Future Goals: What will you do after the degree?
Task: Write a first rough draft — even if it's bad. Use the structure above. Send to John. The first draft is always bad. That is fine. Write it anyway.
Avoid: "I have always been passionate about computers since childhood." | Listing your CV again in paragraph form. | Vague research interests. | Using the same SOP for every university — customise Para 4 each time.
🚀

Module 8: Submitting Your Applications

How to apply smartly and track everything

How Many to Apply To?

Apply to 5–8 universities: 2 stretch schools, 3–4 target schools, 2 safety schools. Prioritise universities where a professor already replied positively to your email.

Application Checklist Per University

  • Create account on the university's online application portal
  • Upload transcripts (sealed or direct registry submission)
  • Upload your CV and customised SOP
  • Enter referees' contact details (they receive an automated email)
  • Upload English test scores
  • Pay application fee (always ask for a waiver first)
  • Submit before the deadline — never on the last day
Track every deadline. Canadian universities typically have January–February deadlines for September intake. Use a spreadsheet. Set phone alarms 30 days and 7 days before each deadline.
🛂

Module 9: Visa & Pre-Departure

What happens after you receive an offer

Never accept an offer without showing John first. Check carefully whether it includes full funding (tuition waiver + stipend) or requires self-funding.

Canadian Study Permit

  • Apply on the IRCC portal (ircc.canada.ca)
  • You will need: acceptance letter, proof of funds (a university funding letter counts), passport, biometrics, medical exam
  • Allow 2–4 months for processing
  • Book your VFS Global appointment in Nigeria early
IRCC Study Permit

🏠 Accommodation

Apply for on-campus housing as soon as you accept your offer — international student housing fills up fast. Check if your university has graduate student housing.

✈️ Arrival

Book your flight only after your visa is confirmed. Note your university's orientation date and arrive before it. Contact the student union for arrival support.
