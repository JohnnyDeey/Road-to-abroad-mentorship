Welcome — Read This First

Who This Is For

You are a graduate or final-year undergraduate who wants to study abroad. Scholarships and funded programmes are the plan.

Good news: Canada, Germany, Norway, and some UK universities offer fully funded graduate programmes. Be strategic.

How to Use This Guide

Go through each Module in order. Check off tasks and add notes — your mentor sees everything live.

Your Mentor Is Watching 👀

Every task you check, every note you write — your mentor sees it in real time.

Key rule: Only apply to funded pathways.
📅

  • Complete Module 1
  • Apply for passport immediately
  • Shortlist 10–15 target universities

Module 1: Know Yourself First

🎓 CGPA

Most funded MSc programmes want 3.0+/5.0.

💻 Projects

List every coding project, FYP, or research.

📖 Publications

Your FYP topic can become your research proposal.

🌍 English Test

IELTS 6.5+ or TOEFL iBT 90+.

Task: Write a 1-page Academic Profile in Google Doc and share the link with your mentor.
🔍

Module 2: Find the Right Programmes

Canada — Research-Based MSc/PhD

Your supervisor funds you — stipend and tuition waiver.

  • Memorial University of Newfoundland
  • University of Manitoba
  • University of Alberta
  • Dalhousie University
  • University of Waterloo
Search: findamasters.com

Germany — Near-Zero Tuition

~€150–350/semester. DAAD scholarships cover living costs.

Australia — Research Training Program

RTP waives tuition for international PhD students.

Norway & Nordic — Free Tuition

Norway's public universities charge no tuition for anyone.

👨‍🏫

Module 3: Find Professors & Read Research

Why This Matters

A professor must agree to supervise you before you are admitted.

How to Find Them

  • Google Scholar: Search your topic
  • ResearchGate
  • University CS department pages
  • IEEE Xplore / arXiv / ACM
Free papers: Sci-Hub.se or arXiv.org.
✉️

Module 4: How to Email a Professor

The Golden Rules

  • Keep it short — 3–4 paragraphs
  • Personalise every email — mention their specific paper by exact title
  • State your CGPA clearly. Attach your CV.
  • Follow up once after 2 weeks if no reply.
📧 Professor Email Template
Subject: Prospective Graduate Student — [Research Area] — [Your Name] Dear Professor [Last Name], I am [Your Name], a final-year Computer Science student at [Your University], graduating in [Month Year] with a CGPA of [X/5.0]. I am writing to inquire about PhD/MSc research opportunities starting [e.g. September 2026]. I recently read your paper "[Exact Paper Title]" ([Year]), and I found your approach to [specific aspect] particularly compelling. My background includes [1–2 sentences on your FYP]. I believe this aligns well with your group's research direction. I have attached my CV. Thank you for your time. Sincerely, [Your Full Name] | [Your University] | [Email]
Never: "I came to know about you through the internet." | Ask about funding in the first email.

Module 6: Documents Checklist

⚠️ Start passport and transcripts today. Nigerian passports take 6–12 weeks.

📘 International Passport

NIS Portal

📋 Full Documents List

  • Official transcripts — sealed copies
  • Academic CV
  • 3 Letters of Recommendation — give 6 weeks notice
  • English Language Certificate
  • Birth Certificate
  • Research Proposal — for PhD
  • Statement of Purpose (SOP)
✍️

Module 7: Writing Your SOP

SOP Structure

  • Para 1 — Hook: Open with a specific CS problem
  • Para 2 — Background: Degree, CGPA, projects
  • Para 3 — Research Interest
  • Para 4 — Why This University/Professor: Name them. Mention their paper.
  • Para 5 — Future Goals
Task: Write a first draft and paste the Google Doc link in your task note.
Avoid: "I have always been passionate since childhood." | Same SOP for every university.
🚀

Module 8: Submitting Applications

How Many?

Apply to 5–8 universities: 2 stretch, 3–4 target, 2 safety.

Per-University Checklist

  • Create account on application portal
  • Upload transcripts + CV + customised SOP
  • Enter referees' contact details
  • Submit before the deadline
🛂

Module 9: Visa & Pre-Departure

Never accept an offer without sharing it with your mentor first.

Canadian Study Permit

  • Apply on the IRCC portal — allow 2–4 months
IRCC Portal

🏠 Housing
